PROJECTS
During our Fall 2023 Internship, we are offering three different research-based projects that encompass the hospital, community, and education settings. Accepted interns are able to choose the rotation of their choice.

Childrens Neurologic Literature

THE PROBLEM
Neurologic education is lacking for uninsured, low-income, and non-English speakers in the US, causing health disparities and misunderstandings on key topics.

WHAT WE'RE DOING
Think Neuro is devoted to delivering this education to overlooked groups. Our programs nurture healthcare advocates for marginalized communities, enhancing research and engagement.

HOW YOU'D HELP
Join our Fall 2023 Internship to address this gap. Engage in our educational and scholarship programs to boost neurologic comprehension where it's crucially needed.

Opioid Epidemic

THE PROBLEM
The opioid crisis has surged globally, resulting in widespread addiction and thousands of fatalities annually. Factors such as overprescription, easy accessibility, and lack of awareness about the dangers of opioids have contributed significantly to this escalating public health challenge.

WHAT WE'RE DOING
Our organization is at the forefront of addressing this issue. We are heavily invested in comprehensive research, promoting responsible prescription practices among healthcare providers, and launching wide-scale awareness campaigns to educate the public about the risks and alternatives.

HOW YOU'D HELP
Research is vital to address the opioid crisis. By supporting our studies, you play a key role in creating effective interventions. Your involvement in our projects and promoting findings drives meaningful change.

Medical Language Exchange

THE PROBLEM
Non-English speaking patients face challenges understanding medical advice, leading to potential misdiagnoses and treatment errors.

WHAT WE'RE DOING
"Medical Language Exchange" aims to bridge this language barrier. We're recruiting bilingual interns to act as mediators, ensuring clear communication between healthcare providers and patients.

HOW YOU'D HELP
By joining, you'll be an essential liaison, translating medical jargon and procedures into the patient's native language. Your role fosters trust, improves patient outcomes, and emphasizes the importance of inclusive healthcare. Together, we ensure no one is left behind due to language barriers.
